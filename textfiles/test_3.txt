A 54-year-old male, with colorectal
adenocarcinoma, after presenting with several months of

Colonoscopy identified a partially obstructing sigmoid

adenocarcinoma. Initial staging with CT imaging of the
chest, abdomen, and pelvis demonstrated no evidence of
distant metastatic disease.

The patient subsequently underwent laparoscopic sigmoid
colectomy with regional lymphadenectomy. Final surgical
pathology showed a tumor invading through the muscularis
propria into the pericolorectal tissues, consistent with
pT3 disease. Four of nineteen examined regional lymph
nodes were positive for metastatic carcinoma,
corresponding to pN2a status. No distant metastases were
identified (M0).

Additional pathologic evaluation demonstrated moderately
differentiated histology with lymphovascular invasion
present. Surgical margins were negative, indicating an R0
resection. Molecular profiling showed the tumor to be
mismatch repair proficient / microsatellite stable
(pMMR/MSS). Genomic testing identified a KRAS G12D
mutation, while NRAS remained wild type and BRAF V600E
was not detected. HER2 testing was negative. Baseline
carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA) at diagnosis was elevated
at 8.6 ng/mL.

Following recovery from surgery, the patient received
standard first-line adjuvant chemotherapy with CAPOX
(capecitabine plus oxaliplatin) for six months.
Treatment was completed with manageable toxicities
including mild peripheral neuropathy and intermittent
gastrointestinal side effects.

During routine surveillance approximately nine months
after completion of adjuvant therapy, serial laboratory
monitoring demonstrated a progressive rise in CEA to
17.4 ng/mL. Restaging CT imaging revealed multiple new
hepatic lesions and enlarged retroperitoneal lymph nodes
suspicious for recurrent metastatic disease. Image-guided
biopsy of a liver lesion confirmed metastatic colorectal
adenocarcinoma consistent with the known primary tumor.

The patient is therefore assessed as having recurrent
metastatic colorectal cancer that has relapsed after
curative-intent surgery and prior oxaliplatin-based
adjuvant chemotherapy. Current disease status is
consistent with chemotherapy-refractory progression
following first-line adjuvant treatment in the setting of
MSS, KRAS-mutated colorectal cancer. The patient is a
candidate for second-line systemic therapy with irinotecan-based treatment such as
FOLFIRI in combination with anti-VEGF therapy.
