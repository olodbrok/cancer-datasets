A 58-year-old woman with a prior history of breast cancer was
initially diagnosed in 2021 with stage IIA hormone receptor-
positive, HER2-negative invasive ductal carcinoma of the left
breast. She underwent lumpectomy with sentinel lymph node biopsy
followed by adjuvant first-line systemic therapy consisting of
docetaxel/cyclophosphamide for 4 cycles and adjuvant endocrine
therapy. Post-treatment imaging and surveillance examinations
demonstrated no evidence of residual or recurrent disease, and she
remained disease-free for approximately 2 years.

In 2023, the patient was diagnosed with colon cancer after
evaluation of abdominal pain and iron-deficiency anemia.
Colonoscopy revealed an ascending colon mass, and biopsy confirmed
moderately differentiated adenocarcinoma. Staging CT showed
localized disease without distant metastases. She underwent right
hemicolectomy, with surgical pathology demonstrating pT3N2a
disease with 5/18 positive lymph nodes.

The patient received first-line adjuvant chemotherapy with FOLFOX
for 6 months. Approximately 14 months later, surveillance imaging
demonstrated multiple hepatic lesions consistent with metastatic
recurrence. Liver biopsy confirmed metastatic colorectal
adenocarcinoma. She subsequently initiated second-line systemic
therapy with FOLFIRI plus bevacizumab.

Follow-up imaging after 8 months of therapy demonstrated interval
enlargement of hepatic metastases and new bilateral pulmonary
nodules consistent with progressive metastatic disease. Current
assessment is metastatic colon cancer with progression following
two prior lines of systemic therapy. ECOG performance status is 1.
